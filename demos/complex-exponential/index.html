How it works.  A pole s = σ + jω generates the mode est = eσt(cos ωt + j sin ωt): a logarithmic spiral that always starts at 1 (because e0 = 1) and whose radius eσt is set purely by the real part while ω sets the rate and direction of rotation. σ < 0 (left half-plane) spirals inward and decays — stable; σ > 0 spirals outward and blows up — unstable; σ = 0 (the jω axis) rides the unit circle forever — marginal. The right panel traces the mode until it leaves the visible window (or the decaying tail vanishes), so zoom the right plot out to see more of a growing spiral, or zoom into the origin to see deeper into a decaying one. The comet shows the direction of increasing t. Turn on + conjugate s̄ to add the mirror pole σ − jω: its trajectory is the mirror image across the real axis, and the two sum to 2eσtcos ωt — the real signal a physical second-order system actually produces.

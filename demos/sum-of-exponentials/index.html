How it works.  f(t) = Σk ckeskt is a sum of phasors. Each term ckeskt is a complex number that rotates at rate ωk = Im sk while its length eσkt grows (σk>0) or decays (σk<0), with σk = Re sk. Drawn tip-to-tail from the origin, the vectors chain into a running partial sum, and the tip of the last vector is f(t) — the number the right-hand plot splits into its real and imaginary parts. A single complex term is never real on its own; the default pairs 0.5 e2jt with its conjugate 0.5 e-2jt — two vectors counter-rotating so their imaginary parts always cancel and the tip slides along the real axis as cos 2t. That is how real signals are built from complex exponentials. Everything runs client-side; nothing touches a server.

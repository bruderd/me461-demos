Nonlinear states are x1,x2 (or x_1,x_2). The transform maps them to the deviation state, written xt1,xt2 (i.e. x̃₁, x̃₂) — use those in the linearization boxes. Functions: sin cos tan exp log sqrt abs sign atan2 …, pi, and ^ for powers. Any other letter (e.g. b, c) becomes a shared tunable parameter with a slider that drives all three systems at once. For a plain shift about an equilibrium x*, set x̃ = x − x* (e.g. x1 - pi).

How it works.  The left column integrates the true nonlinear plant ẋ = f(x). The right column integrates your linearization x̃̇ = A x̃, started from the same physical initial condition mapped through the change of variables x̃ = T(x). Both run on one clock over the shared duration; every trace stays until Clear. The right panel can be viewed in deviation coordinates x̃ (equilibrium at the origin) or mapped back into original coordinates x — in the x-frame the linear trajectory hugs the nonlinear one near the operating point and peels away as you move off it, which is exactly what "linearization is a local approximation" means. Fixed-step RK4; the x-frame mapping inverts T numerically (Newton). All client-side.
